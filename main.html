The Walkman

Purpose of use

The Walkman is a brand of portable media players. It was a portable cassette player that changed listening habits by allowing people to listen to music of their choice on the move.

The cost

After a development phase of only four months, Sony engineers had a reliable product ready for market at 30,000 Yen (approximately $150 in 1979 dollars) and available before the start of the summer vacation for Japanese students.

Its popularity

The initial production run of 30,000 units looked to be too ambitious after the first month of lackluster sales (only 3,000 were sold in July 1979).

The users

Being invented at the end of the 1970s the walkman was used by the 70s and 80s teens. This does not mean that older people couldn’t use it.

Frequency

This device was for sure used a lot while going outside to hang out with friends or during the trip to go to school.
Picture representing the users. Walkman’s battery last, while used a lot, from eight to ten hours even thought it was meant to last forty hours as Sony advertised, but at the same time those hours were enough to listen to music at home and outside.

The style

The Sony’ s walkman was a blue and white box with a little window in the middle through which you could see the cassette type. On the white side there were some buttons which allowed you to skip ahead, stop or skip backwards. At the top there were the 2 jack type connectors for the little headphones provided with the device.

Creation and release date

The word is also a brand; the first specimen was sold on 1st July 1979. A German inventor, Andreas Pavel, in 1980 claimed to be the real inventor, given that two years earlier he had created a very similar object, calling it stereobelt. After several legal proceedings in 1999 Sony and Pavel reached an out-of-court settlement.

What kind of technology was used?

Starting from July 1, 1979 it was discovered that every man was given the prerogative to transform his life into a film, choosing the soundtrack and customizing the rhythm. Until then, the only instrument capable of transmitting music outside was the portable radio, and the arrival of the Walkman irreversibly changed the way we approached music; it had two headphone jacks and a hotline facility to allow language over a microphone.

The device these days.

These days the walkman has been replaced by the mp3 player, later in the years by the mp4 player and the Ipod and even later by the smartphones. So let’s compare these devices: The MP3 was a really little device used like the walkman to listen to music when outside in a comfortable and private way. In fact with all these music players you could choose to listen to some songs by headphones or a speaker. The MP4 was a bit bigger because it used a screen to select and watch music videos while listening to them. In fact the name MP4 such as MP3 came from the extensions used for PC files as music (MP3) and videos (MP4). And after this one the Ipod touch came, a really advanced MP4 made from Apple Inc. a really important and famous tech industry that made and published a lot of technologies such as the mouse and the smartphone or even the portable computer. This device was the closest thing to a smartphone, with a touchscreen, 1 headphone jack, 1 recharge cable and some buttons to low or rise the music volume and the famous HOME button. After seeing all the similarities the Iphone has with the Ipod the idea to put the function to listen to music on the phone came naturally.
